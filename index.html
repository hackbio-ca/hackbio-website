The Toronto Bioinformatics Hackathon is a student-led computational biology hackathon, bringing together early career bioinformaticians, molecular biologists, statisticians, and computer scientists from Torontoâ€™s Discovery District to complete interdisciplinary projects that emphasize computational and entrepreneurial thinking.

In partnership with the Terrence Donnelly Centre and the Temerty Faculty of Medicine's Computational Biology in Molecular Genetics program, our participants will collaborate to propose innovative solutions to key challenges in computational biology with commercialization potential.

How to participate

1. Gather your team

Your team can have 2 - 5 members. Don't forget to pick your Team Lead!

2. Pick a problem

Brainstorm an idea for a project that you'd like to tackle.

3. Submit your project

We vet your projects to ensure they're feasible to complete within a 2-day limit.

4. Get hacking!

Mark your calendars for the big event and we'll see you there!
